Regulator

Dental Board of Australia (DBA)

Sets professional standards and oversees notifications and complaints to protect the public.

Why this matters to you

Your work supports the DBA’s standards, helping maintain patient safety and compliance.

Government Agency

Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra)

Manages the national registration and accreditation scheme and supports the DBA to regulate dentistry.

Why this matters to you

Ahpra handles registrations for the clinicians you assist. Your records and processes reflect Ahpra and DBA expectations for safe practice.

Professional Association

Australian Dental Association (ADA)

The ADA is the peak body for dentists, offering resources, advocacy, and continuing education.

Why this matters to you

ADA guidelines often shape your clinic’s procedures — from infection control to patient communication.

Professional Association

Dental Assistants Professional Association (DAPA)

DAPA supports dental assistants through training, advocacy, and career development opportunities.

Why this matters to you

DAPA provides guidance and CPD opportunities directly aligned to your daily work in dental assisting.

Professional Association

Australian Dental Prosthetists Association (ADPA)

ADPA represents dental prosthetists, advocating for quality, safety, and patient access to services.

Why this matters to you

You may assist prosthetists with impressions, lab coordination, or patient communication—ADPA sets relevant standards.

Professional Associations

DHAA & ADOHTA → Oral Health Association of Australia

The merged body for hygienists and oral health therapists, representing the broader oral health workforce.

Why this matters to you

If your clinic employs hygienists or therapists, their standards and CPD activities influence your role and patient care coordination.
